# Pricing Guidelines for Richmond General

## Margin Targets by Category

| Category | Cost Range | Target Margin | Target Multiplier |
|----------|------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Quick flip items | $1-5 | 150-200% | 2.5-3x |
| Mid-range vintage | $5-25 | 200-300% | 3-4x |
| Showcase pieces | $25+ | 150-400% | Research-based |
| Books (common) | $1-5 | 200-400% | 3-5x |
| Books (collectible) | $10+ | 100-300% | Research-based |
| Carnival glass | $5-50 | 150-250% | 2.5-3.5x |

## Pricing Psychology

### Price Points
- $X.99 for items under $20
- $X.95 or round numbers for $20+
- Avoid $.00 endings (looks lazy)

### Anchoring
- Group similar items at different price points
- Showcase premium items to make mid-range seem reasonable

## Research Sources

### Sold Listings (Most Reliable)
1. **eBay Sold** - Filter "Sold items" in search
2. **Worthpoint** - Historical auction data (subscription)
3. **LiveAuctioneers** - Auction house results

### Listed Prices (Less Reliable)
- eBay current listings (shows asking, not selling)
- Etsy (often inflated)
- Ruby Lane (high-end, inflated)

## Time-Based Pricing

### New Listings
- Price at researched value
- Allow 30 days at full price

### 30-60 Days
- Consider 10-15% reduction
- Add to sale/clearance section

### 60+ Days
- Evaluate: reduce price or hold for right buyer
- Some items are worth waiting for

## Square-Specific

### Pricing Display
- Include full price in listing
- Use "Compare at" for perceived value
- Enable discounts for multi-buy

### Shipping Considerations
- Factor shipping into pricing strategy
- "Free shipping" built into price vs. separate
- Flat rate vs. calculated

## Don't Undersell

**Red flags you're priced too low:**
- Sells within hours of listing
- Multiple inquiries immediately
- Buyer seems too eager

**When in doubt:**
- Price higher, can always negotiate down
- Better to reduce later than leave money on table
- Rare items: research more before pricing
